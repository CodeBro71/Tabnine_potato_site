The Great Irish Potato Famine

History Causes Effects Sources

History

The Great Irish Potato Famine, also known as the Great Hunger, was a period of mass starvation, disease, and emigration in Ireland between 1845 and 1852. It was primarily a consequence of failures in the potato crop, which was a staple food for most of the Irish population at the time.

Causes

The primary cause of the famine was the potato blight, a fungal disease that destroyed the potato crops. Other contributing factors included over-reliance on a single crop, poverty, and a lack of government support and infrastructure to deal with the crisis.

Effects

The famine led to the deaths of approximately one million people and caused another million to emigrate, primarily to the United States and Britain. It had a profound impact on Irish society and culture, and its effects are still felt today.
